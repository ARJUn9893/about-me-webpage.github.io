    #1:BUSSINESS COMMUNICATION

    Business Communication covers a wide range and extends to all functions—purchases, production, sales, finance, recruitment, wages, dividends, market standing, innovation, productivity, etc. It also moves through all levels of management— upward, downward and sideways.

    #2:DATA STRUCTURE

    A data structure is a data organization, management, and storage format that enables efficient access and modification. More precisely, a data structure is a collection of data values, the relationships among them, and the functions or operations that can be applied to the data.

    #3:C PROGRAMMING

    C is a programming language developed at AT & T’s Bell Laboratories of USA in 1972. It was designed and written by a man named Dennis Ritchie. In the late seventies C began to replace the more familiar languages of that time like PL/I, ALGOL, etc

    #4:OPERATING SYSTEM

    An operating system (OS) is system software that manages computer hardware, software resources, and provides common services for computer programs. Time-sharing operating systems schedule tasks for efficient use of the system and may also include accounting software for cost allocation of processor time, mass storage, printing, and other resources.

    #5:DISCRETE MATHEMATICS

    Discrete Mathematics deals with the study of Mathematical structures. It deals with objects that can have distinct separate values. It is also called Decision Mathematics or finite Mathematics . It is the study of mathematical structures that are fundamentally discrete in nature and it does not require the notion of continuity.

    #6:COMPUTER ARCHTECHTURE & ORAGANIZATION

    Computer Architecture refers to those attributes of a system that have a direct impact on the logical execution of a program.
